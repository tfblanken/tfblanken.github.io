During my PhD I collaborated with many different researchers from different research labs, without whom this work would not have been possible.

In the first place my supervisors and their research labs: Eus van Someren, head of the Sleep & Cognition lab; and Denny Borsboom, head of the <a href="http://psychosystems.org/" target="blank">psychosystems group</a>. 

<br/>

<h4>insomnia and depression</h4> 
To identify data-driven subtypes of insomnia disorder we used latent class analysis, for which we collaborated with <a href="https://jeroenvermunt.nl/" target="blank">Jeroen Vermunt</a> (Tilburg University).

To identify sequential and symptom-specific treatment effects we worked together with <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/tanja-van-der-zweerde-4b1b4a40/?originalSubdomain=nl" target="blank">Tanja van der Zweerde</a> (VU University), <a href="https://research.vu.nl/en/persons/annemieke-van-straten" target="blank">Annemieke van Straten</a> (VU University) and <a href="https://www.uva.nl/profiel/l/a/j.lancee/j.lancee.html" target="blank">Jaap Lancee</a> (University of Amsterdam).

To identify primary determinants of depression we worked together with Brenda Penninx, principal investigator of the Netherlands Study of Depression and Anxiety. 


<br/>

<h4>community detection and graph theory</h4> 
Besides my main focus on insomnia and depression, I have developed a great interest in community detection and graph theory. 

I started to explore this field when, together with Marie Deserno, we set up a project to explore the community structure of a large psychopathology network. In this project we collaborated with <a href="https://jdalege.com/" target="blank">Jonas Dalege</a> (University of Amsterdam) and <a href="https://www.aojcramer.com/" target="blank">Angélique Cramer</a> (Tilburg University). 

Afterwards we joined a project led by <a href="https://srebrenkaletina.com/" target="blank">Srebrenka Letina</a> (Central European University, Budapest, Hungary), in which we introduced the applicability of graph theory metrics in psychological networks. 


In a project together with <a href="https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Thomas_Villemonteix" target="blank">Thomas Villemonteix</a> (University 8, Paris, France), we have applied community detection to investigate subtypes of ADHD based on their personality profiles.
